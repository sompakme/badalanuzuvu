The 7 Best Strapless Bras For Small Busts

No wardrobe foundation is complete without a strapless bra. Yet most of us will probably agree that strapless bras can go horribly wrong for any number of reasons. Whether it’s slippage, uni boobs, or what can only be described as the ace bandage effect, finding a good strapless bra is a challenge, to say the least. 

Laura Tempesta, bra expert and founder of Bravolution, says that “smaller busted bodies have a shape under the breasts that are flatter. It’s more like a shallow “U” or a straight line. For that reason, smaller-busted consumers should look for bras with a straighter line design under the bust.” 

For those with small busts, accommodating a strapless look may be easier than it is for your DD counterparts. Though adhesive bras and bandeaus are pretty great, they’re not your only option, nor are they always the best option. The best strapless bras should lift, separate, support the girls, and stay put.

What to Keep in Mind

Check The Cup Shape

Bras with a shallow cup are created for those with a smaller bust, so look for a bra with a subtle cup shape. You don’t need an oversized cup, as it’ll fold over the top of your breast. 

Pay Attention To The Band 

“Make sure that the center front of the band lays flat against your skin,” says Thomas. “You don’t want any gaping in this area; that means your bra band is too large.” 

Moreover, in the absence of straps, the band does the heavy lifting (no pun intended) to support the breasts. For that reason, the band on a strapless bra should be tighter than the band on your regular bra — but don’t pick one so tight it’s uncomfortable.  

Try Before You Buy 

The size and fit often vary from brand to brand, especially for strapless bras. So, to get a perfect fit, you must try the bra before buying it. 

Get a Grip

Slippage is a common concern for strapless bras, so to minimize the possibility of your bra not ending the night where it started, look for a no-slip-grip strapless bra. Plenty of strapless bras come with sturdy silicone on the cups and band to prevent the bra from moving around too much.

Manage Your Expectations 

A strapless bra may never perform or feel like your regular bra. They are different types with different functions, so they shouldn’t feel the same. If you go into the process understanding that from the jump, then you'll be far more likely to succeed in your quest. 

Your Questions, Answered

What makes a good strapless bra?

“Fabrication and the type of construction are all things to look for when searching for a strapless bra,” says Gabrielle Richards, bra expert, body confidence coach, and Senior Brand Manager of Shapermint. 

“The fabric can help determine if it will have stretch, breathability, and be smooth while wearing. And construction is key — if it’s strapless, you are looking for words like boning, anti-slip silicone grip, multiple rows of hook and eye to help adjust the fit, and padded/molded cups to help with the shape.“ 

How do I keep my strapless bra from falling down? 

To ensure that your strapless bra stays where you want it to, you may choose to invest in a high-quality option with a no-slip silicone grip. If the silicone band doesn’t quite work for you, then a long line strapless could be an ideal alternative. It is constructed with longer boning that further stabilizes the bra, so it tends to feel a bit more supportive.

Are strapless bras supportive? 

Contrary to popular belief, strapless bras can be pretty supportive. But whereas the band, the cups, and the straps provide support in traditional bras, their strapless counterparts rely on the band, cups, boning, and larger hooks for support.
